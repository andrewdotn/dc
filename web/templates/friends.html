Sites we love

Here are some of our favourite sites and how they describe themselves:

  • Timetric.com “Everything you need to publish data and research online … Timetric aggregates statistics from the world's leading sources of economic data.” They also have great support for integrating a chart into online media.

  • ScraperWiki is a collaborative platform for writing and scheduling screen scrapers—programs that turn graphical charts and tables into reusable data sources.

  • “Wolfram Alpha’s long-term goal is to make all systematic knowledge immediately computable and accessible to everyone.” A list of subject areas it’s recently learned about is mind-blowing.

  • GapMinder’s goal “is to replace devastating myths with a fact-based world view.” Watch the world-famous TED Talk!

  • Infochimps: “We make data easy to access so you can focus on being more productive.” Instead of toiling for hours to collect, format, and sort data into useful formats, get it instantly from Infochimps, “a place to find, sell and share data with others.”

  • Google’s Public Data Explorer “makes large datasets easy to explore, visualize and communicate” and “[enables] people to better understand the world around them and make more informed, data-driven decisions.”

    The project has proposed a Dataset Publishing Language called DSPL.

  • ManyEyes aims “to ‘democratize’ visualization and to enable a new social kind of data analysis.”

    NYTimes Economix uses ManyEyes.

  • Multpl.com is a brilliantly simple solo effort with 16 high-level econopmic datasets such as historical P/E ratio for US equities.

  • zFacts is a website that hopes to fill a crucial but almost empty niche: To provide think-tank quality information in an easily accessible format … to help the interested public and the press understand current social and political issues.”

  • BuzzData is “a social platform where publishing, discussing and building the story around your hard-earned data is a snap.”

  • Datacurious (in development).

  • iCharts “is a software-as-a-service (SaaS) company that provides the only easy-to-use and affordable online service for interactive chart creation and social sharing.” Their vision is to “to empower the world to explore and share data by enabling the free flow of visual data among data owners, media, business and consumers.”

  • Explainum—“like wiki for charts”—lets you can build live-updating charts, annotate key data points, and embed them in web pages.

  • Verifiable.com had a beautiful UI but is no longer operating. Read the post-mortem.

  • Swivel.com is also no longer operating; read the post-mortem.

  • The iPad app Economy shows the latest and historical values of key US economic indicators. It has has a beautiful and effective API.

  • Numbrary.com is a (possibly-defunct) free online service dedicated to finding, using and sharing numbers on the web.

  • Data360.org’s mission is to tell data-driven stories about important subjects. It’s free and features about 5,000 relevant datasets, with graphing features.

  • NationMaster.com and StateMaster.com offer data on nations and US states.

  • WikiPosit aims to be a Wikipedia of numerical data.

  • Factual.com is an open data platform and community trying to maximize data accuracy, transparency, and availability.

  • GetTheData.org is like Stack Overflow for asking and answering data-related questions.

  • The Socrata Social Data Platform “is the most widely-adopted Open Data solution in Government.”

  • Open Data Directory is a free search engine for data sets published by governments, private companies and other organizations. It now indexes more than 450,000 datasets from many sources.

  • Windows Azure DataMarket: “One stop shop for Data. Get all data you need for your insights: trusted commercial and premium public domain data.”
